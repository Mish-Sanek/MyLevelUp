Privacy Policy

This Policy sets forth the commitment of Refocus E-Learning Philippines Corp. This document explains how Refocus fulfills this obligation and the terms on which we collect and process personal data. When processing personal data, we strive to adhere to the general principles of privacy, such as transparency, legitimate purpose and proportionality, as well as other relevant principles in the collection, processing and storage of personal data, as required by applicable law. This Notice and any updates, changes or additions to it are available at https://refocus.me/.

Your rights under the DPA

Under the DPA, data subjects have the following rights:

Right to object. As a data subject, you have the right to object to the collection and processing of your personal data, including processing for the purposes of direct marketing, automated processing or profiling. You also have the right to be informed and not consent to further processing in the event of any changes or additions to the information provided to you. Once you notify us that you withdraw your consent, further processing of your personal data will be prohibited unless:

  • (i) processing is required in response to a subpoena, lawful order or as required by law; or
  • (ii) collection and processing are carried out in accordance with any lawful basis or criteria that may be permitted under the DPA.

Right of access. Upon your request, you may be given access to your personal data that we collect and process. You also have the right to request access to the circumstances surrounding the processing and collection of your personal data, to the extent permitted by law.

Right to correction. You have the right to challenge any inaccuracy or error in your personal data and may request that we correct it immediately. At your request and after corrections have been made, we will notify any recipient of your personal data about its inaccuracy and subsequent corrections.

Right to deletion or blocking. If there are no other legitimate grounds or overriding legitimate interests for the lawful processing of your personal data, and if there is substantial evidence that your personal data is incomplete, out of date, false or obtained unlawfully, you may request that we suspend, withdraw or dispose of about blocking, deleting or destroying your personal data from our storage system. We may also notify those who previously received personal data processed by you.

Right to compensation. You are entitled to compensation for damages incurred in connection with inaccurate, incomplete, obsolete, false, unlawfully obtained or unauthorized use of your personal data, subject to any violation of your rights and freedoms as a data subject, in accordance with the law.

Transferability of data subject rights. After the death of the data subject, or in the event of incapacity or inability of the data subject to exercise legal rights, the legal heirs and assigns of the data subject may exercise the rights of the data subject in place of the data subject.

Limitation of rights; implementation procedure. The above rights do not apply if personal data is processed only for the purposes of scientific and statistical research, without being the basis for carrying out any activity or making any decision regarding you as a data subject. Your rights as a data subject are subject to other restrictions provided by law.

The law requires that you exercise your rights in this Notice in a manner that is reasonable and not arbitrary and that is sensitive to the rights of other parties
